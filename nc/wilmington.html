Hiring for stairlifts and grab bars in Wilmington

For stairlifts

Ask for an in-home measurement, the lift’s weight limit, battery behavior during an outage, warranty terms, maintenance response time, and a written price that separates equipment from installation. If you are comparing a used or rental unit, confirm which parts and labor are covered and whether the rail is fitted to the specific staircase.

For grab bars and bathroom safety

Placement should match the user’s movement and the wall structure. Ask how the installer will locate framing or add blocking, what fasteners and bar ratings are being used, and whether wall or waterproofing repairs are included. An occupational therapist can help identify useful placement for an individual; this directory does not provide a personalized safety assessment.

Verify contractors and find local assistance

For projects that fall under state contractor requirements, consult the North Carolina Licensing Board for General Contractors. The Cape Fear Council of Governments Area Agency on Aging provides regional aging and disability resources, and New Hanover County’s Adult Enhancement Services publishes local programs and contact information. Confirm eligibility and current program terms directly with each agency.

Compare estimates

Request itemized estimates, current insurance information, recent comparable references, permit responsibility where applicable, and both product and installation warranties. Do not rely on a directory rating alone when making a safety-related hiring decision.

Frequently asked questions — Wilmington

How do I verify a contractor in Wilmington is legitimate?

Ask for proof of licensing and insurance, at least three references from recent aging-in-place jobs, and whether they hold the CAPS credential from the National Association of Home Builders. Check reviews across Google and the BBB — not just one platform.

How fast can grab bars be installed?

Most Wilmington-area handymen and safety installers can complete a multi-bar bathroom installation in a single visit of one to three hours, provided the bars can anchor into studs or solid blocking.

Is a walk-in tub or a curbless shower better?

Curbless (zero-entry) showers suit wheelchair users and tend to help resale value; walk-in tubs suit people who benefit from soaking and hydrotherapy but require sitting in the tub while it fills and drains. A CAPS remodeler can assess which fits the person and the plumbing.
