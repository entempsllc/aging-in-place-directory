About Aging Gracefully

A practical, independent directory and education project for families planning safer, longer life at home.

Last reviewed: July 20, 2026

Who we are

Aging Gracefully is operated by EnTemps LLC. We publish local provider directories and plain-language educational guides for older adults, family caregivers, and people planning home modifications. Questions may be sent to entempsllc@gmail.com.

What we publish

  • Local directories: publicly available contact and service information for home-modification contractors and senior-support organizations.
  • Planning guides: educational overviews that help readers prepare questions for contractors, clinicians, benefits counselors, attorneys, and other qualified professionals.
  • Official resources: links to government agencies and established nonprofit organizations where readers can verify benefits, licenses, safety guidance, and local services.

How directory information is handled

Listings may originate from public business information or provider submissions. We review for obvious category relevance, duplicates, and geographic mismatches, but we do not represent every listed organization as individually screened, licensed, insured, certified, or endorsed. Contact details, service areas, and business status can change. Third-party ratings and review counts are not currently displayed because their source, retrieval date, attribution, and refresh cadence have not been verified. Readers should verify provider details directly before hiring or sharing personal information.

Businesses do not have to pay for a basic listing or correction. Sponsored placement, if offered, is labeled and does not purchase a positive review, a credential claim, or control over factual corrections. Our complete standards are in the editorial and advertising policy.

How guides are prepared

Guides are written and maintained by the Aging Gracefully Editorial Team. We favor primary and authoritative sources, including Medicare, the Department of Veterans Affairs, the National Institute on Aging, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, state licensing agencies, and local government programs. Each guide identifies its review date and key sources. Our team provides general education, not individualized medical, legal, financial, engineering, or contractor advice.

Corrections and accountability

If you find a broken link, unsupported statement, outdated listing, accessibility problem, or other error, use our correction process. Straightforward corrections are not conditioned on payment. We may ask for a public source when a requested change is disputed.

How the site is funded

The site may earn revenue from clearly labeled advertising, sponsored placements, or affiliate links. Revenue supports hosting and maintenance but does not change our correction standards or turn a listing into an endorsement. See our privacy policy for information about advertising technologies.
